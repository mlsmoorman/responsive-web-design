Whitney Houston

Houston performing "I Look to You" on Good Morning America, September 1, 2009
  • 1963: Born in Newark, NJ
  • 1977: Houston places 2nd in statewide talent showcase and sings background for mother's band
  • 1978: Non-church debut at Manhattan's Town Hall singing "Tomorrow" from "Annie"
  • 1980: Sings background vocals for Chaka Khan on their "Naughty" album
  • 1981: Houston records 3 demo albums looking to get a recording contract
  • 1983: Signed to Arista Records
  • 1984: First single released, hitting top 10 on the R&B charts!
  • 1985: "Whitney Houston" was her debut album released in 1985. The album stayed on the Billboard200 for 3 years and would hit #1 a full year after its release date where it stayed for a mind-blowing 14 weeks!
  • 1987: "Whitney" her second album was released. This record stayed on the Billboard200 as the number one song for its first 11 weeks. This record has yet to be beat by any other female recording artist.
  • 1990: Her next album is released: "I'm Your Baby Tonight". This stayed in the top 10 on the Billboard200 for 22 weeks.
  • 1991: Whitney Houston sings the National Anthem for Super Bowl XXV. Her performance reached the Top 20 on the US Hot 100, the biggest chart hit for a performance of the National Anthem.
  • 1992"The Bodyguard" is released. This would be one of the highest grossing films of all time at the time of its release. The single, "I Will Always Love You" hit 4x platinum and and would make her one of 3 female artists to ever earn diamond on a single and an album.
  • 1995: "Waiting to Exhale" is released. "Exhale (Shoop Shoop) was the third single in music history to debut at number one. This was Houston's 11th and final single.
  • 1996: "The Preacher's Wife" is released. The soundtrack was the first gospel album by a female artist to debut at number one on the Billboard Gospel Charts.
  • 1998: "My Love is Your Love" is released.
  • 1999: Houston participates in VH-1's Divas Live '99 and goes on her "My Love is Your Love" tour.
  • 2000: "Whitney: the Greatest Hits" is released worldwide.
  • 2002: The Diane Sawyer interview where Whitney opens up about her marriage, drug abuse and other topics.
  • 2003: Whitney's first Christmas album is released: "One Wish: Holiday Album"
  • 2004: Whitney becomes a prominent figure on "Being Bobby Brown" on Bravo.
  • 2009: "I Look to You" is released.
  • 2012: "Sparkle" is released along with "Celebrate" which would be the last single Whitney would record.
  • 2012: Whitney is found unresonsive in her hotel room and dies at age 48.
